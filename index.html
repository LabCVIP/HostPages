DNA

The Blueprint of Life

🧬

What is DNA?

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the molecule that carries genetic instructions for the growth, development, functioning, and reproduction of all living organisms. It acts as the biological blueprint that determines characteristics such as eye color, height, and many biological processes.

Structure of DNA

Double Helix

DNA has a twisted ladder shape called a double helix. This structure was discovered by scientists James Watson and Francis Crick.

Nucleotides

DNA is made of repeating units called nucleotides. Each nucleotide contains a sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogen base.

Base Pairs

The bases pair in a specific way: Adenine (A) with Thymine (T), and Cytosine (C) with Guanine (G).

Why DNA is Important

Genetic Information

DNA stores the instructions needed to build proteins and control biological functions.

Inheritance

DNA allows traits to be passed from parents to offspring through genes.

Evolution

Changes in DNA sequences over generations contribute to evolution and biological diversity.

Fun Facts

  • If stretched out, the DNA in one cell would be about 2 meters long.
  • The human genome contains about 3 billion base pairs.
  • Nearly every cell in your body contains the same DNA.
